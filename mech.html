Mechanical Team

Mechanical Team Overview

The structure of the AUV is a basic rectangular prism shape with a cylindrical electronics housing inside. The main structure will be made of extruded T-slotted aluminum to allow fast and easy adjustments to the structure. The material being used is 6061 aluminum which has good corrosion resistance, a good strength to weight ratio, and is cost effective. A custom frame 3-D printed out of ABS plastic will hold all of the electronics in place. The AUV will be propelled through water using four BlueRobotics T200 thrusters, which can provide 7.8 lbf at 12V. The thrusters will be placed to allow three degrees of freedom. The AUV will be trimmed to a neutral buoyancy so that the thrusters will not have to run constantly to keep it at the needed depth. All external wiring will be fed into the electronics enclosure using custom-made cable penetrators that will allow the cables inside the housing without compromising the water -tight properties of the tube. Water-tight enclosures have been designed for the sensors that are not waterproof.
